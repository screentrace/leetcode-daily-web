CGPA for Big Tech Placement: Requirements, Cutoffs & Myths

May 24, 2026 7 min read Campus Placement

Final-year students obsess over CGPA - and misinformation spreads fast. "Google needs 9.0." "Amazon rejects below 7.5." "Backlogs mean game over." Some of this is true for specific campus drives; much of it is outdated or company-specific. Here is a clear breakdown for 2026 placement season.

Mass Recruiters vs Product Companies

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant, and similar mass hirers often enforce 60–70% aggregate cutoffs and zero active backlogs at offer letter time. These filters happen before you ever see a coding problem - aptitude and English rounds eliminate many candidates here.

Product companies (Google, Amazon, Microsoft, startups) vary. Campus shortlists at tier-1 colleges may filter at 7.5–8.0 for initial resume screens. Off-campus applications frequently have no published GPA gate - performance in OA and interviews decides.

Low CGPA? What Actually Helps

  • Strong GitHub and portfolio projects referenced on resume
  • Competitive OA scores that force interview regardless of GPA
  • Internship at recognizable company or meaningful open source
  • Referrals from employees who can advocate for skills over grades
  • Off-campus applications where resume filters are less rigid

Do Not Let GPA Become an Excuse

Students with 6.8 CGPA land product offers every year - usually through off-campus, referrals, and months of daily DSA. Students with 9.0 CGPA fail OAs because they never practiced. Fix what you control: one problem daily on LeetCode Daily, clean resume, parallel applications. See campus vs off-campus for channel strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What CGPA is required for Google placement?

Google does not always publish a strict cutoff, especially off-campus. Strong interview performance can compensate. Some campus drives filter at 7.0–8.0 depending on college.

Can I get placed with backlogs?

Mass recruiters often require zero active backlogs at offer time. Product companies may be flexible off-campus if you clear interviews before graduation.

Does CGPA matter after the first job?

Rarely. After your first role, experience and interview performance dominate. Use final year to maximize skills even if your GPA is not perfect.
