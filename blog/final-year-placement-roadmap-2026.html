Final Year Campus Placement Roadmap 2026: Big Tech Edition

May 24, 2026 10 min read Campus Placement

Final year feels like a race you did not sign up for: college projects, exams, and placement season arriving at once. If you are targeting Google, Amazon, Microsoft, or similar product companies, random preparation will not work. You need a timeline that matches when companies actually hire - and what they test at each stage.

This roadmap is built for 2026 batch students in India and similar markets where campus drives peak mid-year and Big Tech runs rolling off-campus windows. Follow it month by month, adjust for your college calendar, and pair every phase with daily coding practice through LeetCode Daily.

January–February: Foundation and Applications

Product companies begin closing prior campus cycles and opening off-campus New Grad roles. If you are pre-final year reading ahead - start now. Final-year students should:

  • Register on company career portals (Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, etc.)
  • Finalize a one-page resume with two strong projects
  • Solve one DSA problem daily at your skill level
  • Review OS, DBMS, and networking basics for MCQ rounds

March–April: Internship Converts and Startup Wave

Intern return offers roll out. Mid-tier startups and unicorns open SDE roles. Lock in a daily habit - streaks matter more than heroic weekends. Use LeetCode Daily reminders so prep survives end-semester exam pressure.

May–July: Mass Recruiter Registration + Product OA Season

TCS NQT, Infosys, and similar tests open - register even if Big Tech is your goal (backup offers reduce anxiety). Product companies run online assessments testing two to three coding problems plus CS fundamentals. Focus areas: trees, graphs, sliding window, and DP.

August–October: Peak Campus Drive Season

Tier-1 and tier-2 campuses host product and mass recruiters. You should already have 60+ reviewed problems under your belt. Run weekly timed mocks. Before each OA, sleep well - fatigue kills more offers than missing one obscure algorithm.

November–February: Off-Campus Second Wave

Missed campus? Off-campus portals stay active. Referrals pick up. Keep applying weekly. Students who maintain daily practice through LeetCode Daily often report this period is when months of streaks pay off - interviews feel familiar, not panic-inducing.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should final year students start placement prep?

Start DSA fundamentals in pre-final year if possible. By July–August before placement season, you should be solving daily medium problems and have two portfolio projects ready.

Which month do Big Tech companies hire freshers?

Google, Amazon, and Microsoft often run campus and off-campus cycles between August and February. Mass recruiters peak June–October. Apply early - roles fill on rolling basis.

How many hours per day should I study for campus placement?

Two to three focused hours daily beats eight unfocused hours. One LeetCode-style problem plus CS fundamentals review is a sustainable final-year schedule alongside college.
